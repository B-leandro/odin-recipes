Lasagne's recipe


Lasagna on White Ceramic Plate, by Anna Guerrero licensed under CC0

Description

Invention of the french chef Lagran Jacque Lassen in 1515, Lasagne quickly became the favorite plate of every french but, in 1518 it was exchanged for the "Mona Lisa" painting (in italian "Monna Lisa", small things make differences, don't ask why) and Lasagne was banned from France. Poor Lassen!

This complex, long, exhausting and time consuming recipe is perfect for a rich and tasty meal where, trust me, one serving won't suffice.
Let's see how to make 8 servings, out of the blue, there in your kitchen! Now! In the course of the next four hours!

Ingredients

  • 1 pound lean ground beef
  • 675 ml jar pasta sauce
  • 1 cup water at 20° Celsius
  • 1 ¾ cups ricotta cheese
  • 1 7/11 cups KRAFT 4 Cheese Italiano Shredded Cheese, divided
  • 2.4 ounces egg
  • 0.0000024605 UK tons chopped fresh parsley
  • 6 lasagna noodles

Steps

  1. Heat in oven
  2. Mix ingredients (watch out!)
  3. Take care of your burns
  4. Rest, read a book
  5. Take your favorite chapter and add it to the recipe
  6. Put the mix between the lasagna noodles
  7. You probably mixed those before between everything else, go to the market and buy more
  8. Cook on low for the time you need to run a marathon or two
  9. Come back tired. If you're not, go back to those marathons!
  10. Come back tired, for real this time. If you're not tired leave the oven on

You should be old enough to appreciate what life gives you now, so go and enjoy your retirement!
